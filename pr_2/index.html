Baltimore Trash Museum

web design . project 2 . by jack davia

Project Intro & Guidelines:

Create a three-page website (Home, About, Individual Listing) for a fictional museum of our choosing. This collection is to pertain to given module shapes, those being a triangle, circle, or plus-sign (cross). Modules are intended to be explored in different ways, utilizing repetition, distortion, figure/grounding, clipping, rotation, colorization, etc. Find aspects of the module to construct a site-wide grid, as well as clusters of information within each page. Select typographic choices that strengthen or compliment the visual theme that has been chosen. The final prototype must contain the chosen module at three levels of scale to reinforce a triad of hierarchy. The constraints for this project are: 1) Pages must include a mixture of type and image. 2) All pages must include a main navigation menu, page title, logomark and rotating gallery of collection. They may also include blocks of text and other information to fill out proposed information architecture. 3) Grid must conform to a 12- or 16- column 960px grid.

I am going to go around to various garbage cans around Baltimore City and extract the trash from them and document them. I want to get some places that are equidistant from each other to get a wider variety of things that can be thrown away in that area. For example, if I get a garbage can right around a McDonald's, I will mostly just find crumbled up burger bags and wrappers. I will have to look at a map and plot it out. However, I want to document trash from a different section of the city. This will include, thus far, Downtown, Inner Harbor, Charles Village, Bolton Hill, Hollin's Market, Remmington, Hampden, Pigtown, Fells Point, Canton, Highlandtown, and Lexington Market.

Each unit will contain an aerial shot of each garbage can. Within that, each item will be individually broken up and photographed. The location of each can will be the top hierachy, and the titles of the objects under that. I will establish some qualitative variables for each item, but those have yet to be decided. There will probably be three to four for each. I think having a map of a radius around each garbage can I select will tie in nicely to the module as well.
